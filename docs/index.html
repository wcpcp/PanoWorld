Abstract

Multimodal large language models still struggle with spatial understanding under the dominant perspective-image paradigm. While 360° panoramic sensing captures the entire surrounding environment at once, existing MLLM pipelines typically decompose panoramas into multiple perspective views and leave the spherical structure of ERP imagery largely implicit.

PanoWorld studies pano-native understanding, where an MLLM reasons over an ERP panorama as a continuous, observer-centered space. The project introduces: a capability-based formulation of panoramic spatial understanding; a large-scale metadata construction pipeline that converts mixed-source panoramas into geometry-aware instruction tuning data; a pano-aware MLLM with Spherical Spatial Cross-Attention; and PanoSpace-Bench, a diagnostic benchmark for ERP-native spatial reasoning.

What This Project Contributes

Capability Taxonomy

A structured decomposition of pano-native learning into spherical localization, relative direction reasoning, viewpoint transformation, 3D spatial relations, and global scene topology.

570K Panorama Resource

A metadata-driven pipeline that constructs large-scale geometry-aware and language-grounded ERP supervision from mixed-source panorama data.

PanoSpace-Bench

A diagnostic benchmark that evaluates ERP-native spatial reasoning beyond standard VQA accuracy, including localization, seam awareness, and 3D spatial understanding.

Spherical Spatial Cross-Attention

A pano-aware architectural adaptation that injects spherical geometry directly into the visual stream for geometry-consistent panoramic reasoning.

Method Overview

PanoWorld augments a panoramic MLLM with spherical geometry and pano-native supervision instead of relying on fragmented perspective crops.
